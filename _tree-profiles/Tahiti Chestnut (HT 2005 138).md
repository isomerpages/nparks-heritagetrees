<p>A medium sized evergreen tree that grows up to 30 m in height, with a crown diameter of 4.6 m. Likely from eastern Malesia.  
  
</p><p>The seed is edible when cooked and is among the most important nut species in the Pacific. The wood is also used for crafts, tool handles, canoes and light construction. Currently, Tahiti Chestnut is the only edible and culturally important species in the genus <em>Inocarpus</em>. 
	
</p><p>This species of tree was introduced into the Gardens in 1876 and this specimen most likely dates back to the original introduction. This Heritage Tree had a girth of 4 m when measured in Year 2005.</p>
	
<p>For more information on the species, please refer to NParks Flora &amp; Fauna's profile <a href="https://www.nparks.gov.sg/florafaunaweb/flora/4/3/4327">here</a></p>
